More info equals greater power. You have to not only eliminate the noise to news ratio but also consolidate the flood of information to a very narrow stream that is focused on the topics of specific to your interest. The TheOnion.com or Slashdot.org have lots of info but it is like a shotgun rambling off on thousands of topics. No focus there except when you drill down to a specific category of topics that interest you. And even then there is a lot of garbage blended in compared to solid facts. SecurityNewsPortal.com provides a focus specifically on security related news. But even within that niche of security news there is room for information overload when you are only interested in topics about vulns, or anonymity or privacy for example. Its great for those who want the full overview of the security scene, but too much noise if you want just news about privacy. But the problem is that when you go to web sites that are specific to some subgroup of a niche, you often are exposed to the information pickings of one webmaster. Even on a topic like anonymity you end up seeing just the info chosen by that webmaster. So information overload is good.
